6.2.1
Computing Credible Intervals from a Bayes’ Box
The method for computing credible intervals is closely related to the method for computing
the posterior median. With the median, we found the value of
θ
which has 50% of the
posterior probability to its left and 50% to its right. To ﬁnd the lower end of a 95%
credible interval, we ﬁnd the
θ
value that has 2.5% of the probability to its left. To ﬁnd
the upper end we ﬁnd the value of
θ
that has 2.5% of the posterior probability to its right,
or 97.5% to the left.
6.2.2
Computing Credible Intervals from Samples
If you have used MCMC and have obtained random samples from the posterior distribution,
you can ﬁnd a credible interval in a similar way to how you would ﬁnd the posterior median.
Again, instead of ﬁnding the 0.5 quantile of the posterior distribution you would ﬁnd the
0.025 quantile and the 0.975 quantile (if you wanted a central 95% credible interval).
